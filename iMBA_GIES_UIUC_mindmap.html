iMBA Student — Platform Navigation Guide

Gies College of Business · University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

📚

Canvas — Your Academic Hub

illinois.instructure.com · Primary LMS

▼
When to use Canvas: Every week, every day. Canvas is your central home for all iMBA coursework. Check it first thing each week to review announcements, upcoming deadlines, and new module content. All official course materials, grades, and communications live here — not on email.
📋
Syllabus
▶
Read First · Week 1
Read the full syllabus on Day 1 — it is the contract between you and the professor
Check grading weights: participation, quizzes, assignments, exams, and group projects
Note the late-submission policy and academic integrity rules specific to that course
Identify required textbooks, materials, or software licenses needed
Mark office hours, teaching team contacts, and response-time expectations
Review the course schedule — map all due dates to your personal calendar immediately
Look for participation-point breakdown (Yellowdig posts, discussion boards, etc.)
📢
Announcements
▶
Check Daily
Turn on Canvas push/email notifications for announcements — these are time-sensitive
Professors post deadline changes, clarifications, and extra-credit opportunities here
Check at the start of every week and again mid-week
Look for live session links (Zoom) that may be shared here rather than the syllabus
TA office hours updates and substitute instructor notices appear here first
Scroll back through old announcements if you missed context from a prior week
📦
Modules
▶
Weekly Structure
Each week is organized as a Module — start here every Monday to see what's due
Modules contain: Coursera MOOC links, readings, lecture slides, assignments, and quizzes
Complete items in order — Canvas may lock later items until prerequisites are done
Look for "Weekly Overview" or "Learning Objectives" pages at the top of each module
Download slides and resources for offline study before travel or poor-connectivity weeks
Some modules have "optional" enrichment content — mark these for when bandwidth allows
🎥
MOOCs (Coursera Links)
▶
Linked from Canvas
Canvas modules contain direct links to Coursera video lectures — follow these links first
Completion of Coursera videos is tracked and may count toward participation or prerequisite gating
Do NOT look for videos in Canvas itself — they live on Coursera; Canvas just points there
Some quizzes inside Coursera are graded through Canvas automatically via integration
Always complete MOOC videos before attempting Canvas assignments or discussions for that week
📝
Assignments
▶
Submit Here
All written assignments, case analyses, and team deliverables are submitted here
Check the rubric before starting — it is attached to every assignment and shows exact grading criteria
Note whether it's individual or group — group submissions usually require one designated submitter
Turnitin plagiarism check is embedded — submit early to review your similarity report
Use the "Add a Comment" box to leave notes for the grader if needed
Download the submission confirmation PDF as proof of on-time submission
Check "Assignment" view (not just Modules) for a master list sorted by due date
✅
Quizzes
▶
Timed · No Retakes
Quizzes are often timed (10–30 min) and have only one attempt — read the instructions carefully
Complete all Coursera MOOC readings and videos before starting any quiz
Quizzes may auto-submit when the timer ends — ensure stable internet connection
Some quizzes allow open-notes — confirm this in the quiz instructions, not the syllabus
Check whether Canvas quiz or Coursera quiz — they're different and both may exist in a course
Review incorrect answers after grading; some professors provide answer explanations
Proctored exams may use Honorlock — ensure camera, mic, and browser extension are ready
💬
Discussions
▶
Graded Participation
Canvas Discussions are formal, graded threads — different from Yellowdig (casual community)
Post your initial response by mid-week (usually Wednesday) to leave time for peer responses
Respond substantively to at least 2–3 peers; avoid one-liners like "Great point!"
Reference course concepts, readings, or real-world examples to earn full rubric marks
Check the grading rubric — some discussions grade on depth, citations, and peer replies separately
Subscribe to threads so you're notified when peers reply to your post
🐝
Yellowdig
▶
Community · Points
Yellowdig is the social learning community embedded in Canvas — think academic social media
Points are earned by posting, commenting, liking, and reacting — not just reading
Yellowdig has a weekly point cap and a total semester cap — spread engagement consistently
Post industry articles, personal experiences, or questions related to course themes
Reply to classmates' posts across different time zones — iMBA is global, so anytime is prime time
Avoid spam: quality posts (3–5/week) beat volume; low-effort posts earn minimal points
Yellowdig points feed into your Canvas participation grade automatically
Use it to network — many study groups and peer connections form here organically
📊
Grades
▶
Track Weekly
Canvas Grades shows your running total — check after every submission is graded
Use "What-If" grade calculator to simulate how remaining assignments affect your final grade
Read instructor feedback in the SpeedGrader comments — not just the numeric score
If a grade seems incorrect, contact the TA first, then escalate to the professor within the regrade window
Grade column colors: green (above average), yellow (average), red (below average) — monitor red items
Final grades may differ from Canvas if the professor applies a curve or adjusts weights
Unofficial grades appear here; official transcript grades are on Student Self-Service (Banner)
🖼️
Course Gallery
▶
Media Library
Course Gallery (or Media Gallery) hosts recorded Zoom sessions, instructor video lectures, and supplemental media
Check here for recorded office hours — invaluable if you missed live sessions due to time zone
Instructor-recorded explainers and walkthrough videos are posted here throughout the semester
Videos have closed captions — enable them for complex lectures or when studying in noisy environments
You can add video comments at specific timestamps — use this to ask time-specific questions
Some courses allow students to post their own video responses here for presentations
Download recordings before they expire — Zoom cloud recordings may be deleted after the semester
⚡ Canvas Pro Tip: Set your Canvas notification preferences to "ASAP" for Announcements and Grading, and "Daily Summary" for Discussions. Use the Canvas mobile app for on-the-go check-ins, but always submit major assignments from a desktop browser to avoid upload issues.
🎓

Coursera — Video Learning Engine

coursera.org · MOOC Content Delivery

▼
When to use Coursera: Before Canvas assignments and quizzes each week. Coursera hosts all video lectures, reading materials, and some embedded quizzes. Access it through Canvas module links (not directly via coursera.org search) to ensure you're in the iMBA-enrolled version of the course.
🔗
Access via Canvas
▶
Always Link from Canvas
Never search Coursera directly — always click from Canvas to ensure you're in the right cohort
Use your @illinois.edu email to authenticate; do not create a separate free Coursera account
Completion data syncs back to Canvas only when accessed through the LTI-linked pathway
🎬
Video Lectures
▶
Core Content
Watch all required videos before any Canvas quiz, assignment, or discussion for that week
Use playback speed (1.25×–1.5×) for conceptual review; slow down for quantitative/formula segments
Enable subtitles and use the transcript panel for keyword search within long lectures
Take notes inside Coursera's note-taking feature — notes are saved and searchable later
Download videos for offline viewing during travel (Coursera mobile app required)
📖
Readings & Resources
▶
Don't Skip
PDF readings, case studies, and supplemental articles live alongside videos in each module item
Readings often contain exam-testable content not covered in video lectures
Check if readings are marked "Required" vs. "Optional" — prioritize required in crunch weeks
🧠
Coursera Quizzes
▶
Practice & Graded
Practice quizzes: unlimited attempts, not graded — use them as knowledge checks before graded quizzes
Graded quizzes: usually 1–3 attempts; scores flow to Canvas gradebook automatically
Confirm which platform hosts the "official" quiz — some courses use Canvas quizzes instead
📅
Pacing & Deadlines
▶
Sync with Canvas Calendar
Coursera may show its own suggested deadline — always defer to Canvas for official due dates
Mark videos as "complete" to unlock next items; some courses gate content sequentially
Plan to finish all Coursera content by Thursday so Friday/weekend is for Canvas deliverables
📱
Mobile & Offline Use
▶
Commuter-Friendly
Download the Coursera mobile app and pre-download weekly videos every Sunday night
Ideal for commutes, flights, gym sessions — turn dead time into lecture time
Mobile quizzes work but use desktop for any graded assessment to avoid session drops
⚡ Coursera Pro Tip: Use the Coursera transcript panel (click the speech-bubble icon) to search for specific terms across an entire course. This is a hidden gem during exam prep — search "NPV" or "regression" to jump to every moment a concept was taught.
📹

Zoom — Live Collaboration Layer

zoom.us · Synchronous Sessions

▼
When to use Zoom: For all live, synchronous touchpoints — live class sessions, office hours, group project meetings, guest speakers, and TA review sessions. Links are shared via Canvas Announcements or the syllabus. Always test your audio/video setup 5 minutes before joining.
🏛️
Live Class Sessions
▶
Attendance May Be Graded
Some iMBA courses have optional but strongly recommended live sessions — check syllabus for graded attendance
Arrive 5 min early; use a wired connection or strong WiFi — avoid mobile hotspot for live sessions
Enable your camera when called on or during discussions — professional presence matters in iMBA
Use the "Raise Hand" feature and chat box to participate without interrupting the professor
Sessions are typically recorded and posted to Canvas Course Gallery — but live participation gives Q&A access
🕐
Office Hours
▶
Underused Goldmine
Attend TA and professor office hours weekly — not just when stuck; build a relationship early
Come with specific questions prepared — vague questions get vague answers
Some professors use Calendly for 1:1 slots — book these for grade disputes or career advice
Office hours are held on the same recurring Zoom link — save it in your calendar from Week 1
👥
Group Project Meetings
▶
Team Collaboration
Use Zoom Breakout Rooms or create your own group Zoom meeting for team work sessions
Record your group meetings (with consent) — valuable for absent members and accountability
Use Zoom Whiteboard for brainstorming frameworks, org charts, or strategy maps together
Schedule recurring team meetings in Zoom and sync to Google/Outlook Calendar from Week 1
Use Zoom's screen-share to co-edit Google Docs, Excel models, or slides in real-time
⚙️
Tech Setup & Etiquette
▶
Prepare Before Class
Always use an external microphone or quality headset — laptop mics create echo and background noise
Use a virtual or blurred background if your environment isn't camera-ready
Keep Zoom desktop client updated — in-browser Zoom lacks screen share and whiteboard features
Test audio/video in "Settings → Test Speaker & Microphone" before every important session
Mute yourself when not speaking; use "Reactions" for non-verbal engagement
🎙️
Guest Speakers & Panels
▶
Networking Opportunity
Gies frequently brings in executives and alumni for live Zoom panels — treat these as networking events
Prepare 2–3 thoughtful questions in advance; asking in the Q&A makes you memorable
Connect with speakers on LinkedIn immediately after the session while you're still fresh in their memory
Sessions are usually recorded — share the recording link when updating your study group who missed it
📼
Recordings & Playback
▶
Canvas Course Gallery
Missed a live session? Check Canvas Course Gallery within 24–48 hours for the recording
Watch at 1.25× speed to catch up efficiently — most iMBA recordings have auto-captions
Recordings expire — download or watch within the semester; don't rely on them being available post-finals
Zoom cloud recordings auto-upload to Canvas when the instructor enables the integration
⚡ Zoom Pro Tip: Install Zoom on your laptop AND phone. If your internet drops mid-session, instantly rejoin via mobile hotspot. Also, use Zoom's "Focus Mode" during self-study calls with classmates — everyone sees the host's screen but not each other's, reducing social distraction.
🏫

Student Portal — Administrative Command Center

my.illinois.edu / Banner Self-Service · Registration & Finance

▼
When to use the Student Portal: At the start of every semester for registration, before every payment deadline for tuition, and whenever you need official academic records, financial aid status, or university services. The portal is your administrative backbone — separate from Canvas, Coursera, and Zoom, which are academic delivery tools.
📅
Course Registration
▶
Every Semester
Log in to Student Self-Service (Banner) via my.illinois.edu to register for iMBA courses each term
Check your enrollment appointment time — iMBA students have a designated registration window
Search courses by subject "MBA" or section number provided by Gies iMBA advising team
Add courses to your "Preferred Sections" list before your appointment opens to act fast when registration begins
Some iMBA courses require instructor or advisor approval — submit consent requests early
Verify credit hours and section numbers carefully — online sections are labeled differently from on-campus
Drop/add deadlines are strict — missing them results in "W" (Withdrawal) on your transcript
Contact iMBA advising (imba@illinois.edu) if you encounter registration holds or course conflicts
💳
Tuition & Fee Payment
▶
Bill Due Each Semester
View your tuition bill in the Student Account Center (linked from my.illinois.edu)
iMBA tuition is charged per credit hour — confirm your enrolled hours match your expected bill
Payment methods: eCheck (ACH), credit/debit card (processing fee applies), or wire transfer for international students
Enroll in a payment plan if needed — UIUC offers installment options each semester
Late payments incur a $50–$200 late fee — calendar your due date immediately after billing opens
Employer tuition reimbursement: request a deferment letter from the Bursar's office if your company pays after grades are posted
International students: use Flywire or the university's designated wire transfer portal to avoid bank fees
Check for any "holds" on your account — unpaid balances block future registration and transcript requests
🎓
Financial Aid & Scholarships
▶
Check Every Term
View financial aid awards, loans, and scholarships in the Student Self-Service portal under "Financial Aid"
Accept or decline loan offers each semester — aid does not auto-apply without your acceptance
Check Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) requirements — falling below GPA thresholds can jeopardize aid eligibility
iMBA-specific scholarships may be administered through the Gies Graduate Programs Office — check their website separately
Submit FAFSA annually (for US students) by priority deadlines — typically October 1 for the following academic year
Tax documents (1098-T) for tuition deductions are available in the portal each January
📄
Transcripts & Enrollment Verification
▶
Official Records
Request official transcripts through the National Student Clearinghouse or directly via my.illinois.edu
Unofficial transcripts are free and instant via Student Self-Service — use for personal tracking only
Enrollment verification letters (for employers, visa purposes, loan deferments) are available via the Clearinghouse
Official transcripts take 3–7 business days — plan ahead for job applications and scholarship submissions
Degree verification for post-graduation: UIUC uses the National Student Clearinghouse as the authorized verifier
Grade changes or late grade postings may delay transcript accuracy — wait 2 weeks after semester end before ordering
🪪
NetID, i-Card & Account Access
▶
Set Up Day 1
Your NetID (e.g., jsmith5) is your master university login — used for Canvas, email, library, VPN, and the portal
Activate your NetID and set up 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) via Duo Mobile before orientation
Your @illinois.edu email is provisioned automatically — check it daily as all official university communications go here
i-Card is your university ID — iMBA online students can request a virtual i-Card for library and software access
Reset NetID password at identity.uillinois.edu — do not wait until locked out during an exam period
Access Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, Teams) for free through your Illinois account at office365.illinois.edu
📚
Library & Research Access
▶
Free & Powerful
Access the University of Illinois Library (library.illinois.edu) with your NetID — one of the largest academic libraries in the US
Business databases available: Bloomberg, IBISWorld, Mergent, Statista, EBSCO Business Source Complete
Use the library's VPN (Cisco AnyConnect) when off-campus to unlock full database access
Illinois researchers have access to HBR, WSJ, and FT digital editions at no cost via library subscriptions
Interlibrary Loan (ILL): request any article or book not in the collection — typically delivered in 24–72 hours
Librarians offer 1:1 research consultations — especially useful for capstone projects and literature reviews
🧭
Academic Advising & Degree Audit
▶
Track Your Progress
Use the Degree Audit tool (DARS / DegreeWorks) in the portal to track which requirements you've completed
Schedule a semester check-in with your iMBA academic advisor before registration each term
Confirm your degree concentration courses are correctly mapped — electives don't always auto-populate
Apply for graduation through the portal at least one semester before your expected completion date
Petition for course substitutions or waivers through the advising office — not self-service
iMBA advisors also help with questions on course sequencing, transfer credit, and program milestones
🌐
International Student Services
▶
Non-US Students
ISSS (International Student and Scholar Services) manages visa letters, I-20s, and immigration-related documents
iMBA is a fully online program — international students outside the US typically do not require an F-1 visa
If attending any on-campus residency or event, contact ISSS for guidance on entry requirements and documentation
Tax filing for international students: UIUC provides Glacier Tax Prep software access via the portal
Currency and payment: use Flywire for international tuition payments to avoid SWIFT wire fees
🏥
Health Insurance & Wellness
▶
Optional Enrollment
iMBA students may be eligible to enroll in the Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP) — check the portal each semester
Waive SHIP if you have comparable private coverage — waiver deadline is typically within the first 2 weeks of the semester
Counseling Center (counselingcenter.illinois.edu) offers free teletherapy sessions to enrolled students
McKinley Health Center offers telehealth appointments for enrolled iMBA students regardless of location
Student Assistance Center (SAC) helps with personal emergencies, medical withdrawals, and crisis support
💼
Career Services & Alumni Network
▶
Gies Career Hub
Access Gies Career Services through the portal — includes resume reviews, mock interviews, and career coaching
Handshake (joinhandshake.com) is linked from the portal — use it to search for full-time and internship roles
iMBA students have access to the Illinois Alumni Network (illinoisalumni.org) upon enrollment — not just at graduation
LinkedIn Learning is available for free via your NetID — use it to earn certifications relevant to your concentration
Gies hosts virtual career fairs and company info sessions — check the Events calendar in the portal each semester
Register for the Gies iMBA Slack workspace — an informal but active channel for job leads, peer referrals, and networking
⚡ Portal Pro Tip: Bookmark my.illinois.edu and set a calendar reminder 30 days before each semester's tuition due date and 2 weeks before registration opens. Many iMBA students miss key deadlines simply because the portal feels "administrative" — but a late payment hold can block you from registering for your next semester's courses.
